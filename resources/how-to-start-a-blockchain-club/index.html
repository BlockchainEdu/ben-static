Introduction

While it is not necessary to start or be a part of a blockchain club at your school to be a BEN member, it is a great way to bring together your local community to learn and teach each other about blockchain technology. BEN has helped start and grow blockchain clubs at universities across the world and can provide a lot of guidance in organizing your club.

Club Guidelines

Club Guidelines

  • The club leaders should be currently enrolled students.
  • Set up a new entity at the college, officially registered to the College
  • 2 members as the core team, 1 member in training (May take 6 months when starting a new club) and at least one member should be technical
  • A plan of action (manifesto, analysis of the state of blockchain in that region, what your regional chapters would like to do)
  • Sign a no harassment policy
  • Aim to hold at least one meetup every two months.
  • Commit to sharing resources and materials for other clubs than need it.
  • Onboard all your members as BEN members.
  • Keep BEN up to date with your events, goals, plans, and changes in leadership

Responsibilities of a Club Leader

  • Drive the blockchain community at the college
  • Club meetings weekly or bi-weekly
  • Every meetup is well defined
  • Agenda is required → share with core team
  • Have the members well informed and share some resources about the next agenda

Before and After First Meeting

Before and After First Meeting

  • Before the FIRST meeting, ask students to watch “Mastering Bitcoin”
  • https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=l1si5ZWLgy0
  • AT THE FIRST Meeting - ask who watched the video?
  • Those who raised their hands are your first movers
  • After the first meeting, ask students to watch Mastering Bitcoin
  • First two months
  • Educate members on Blockchain Fundamentals and its inner workings
  • Lead a lesson from our classes below
  • After two months
  • Tackle on plays from the “plays” section
  • We will work together to figure out which blockchain students want to learn about and partner with said blockchain
  • Student leaders will be encouraged to experiment with blockchain technologies and write about them
  • Club meeting
  • Club Leaders will lead technical and non-technical workshops.
